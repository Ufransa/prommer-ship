Talking Points — Agentic Operations

  1. Agentic operations, not agentic chat

    Flywheel Studio already builds on spec-driven development and agentic coding workflows — the shift from AI as a conversational assistant to AI that executes the process end to end.

  2. AI-native, not AI-retrofitted

    We The Flywheel is built as an AI-native digital group from the ground up — designed to move from prototype to production at startup speed, not a legacy org with an AI layer bolted on.

  3. From CTO to CTAIO

    One of Prommer's standing keynote topics: what changes when the technology leadership role expands to own AI strategy, not just infrastructure.

  4. Responsible AI in practice

    Another of his core talks: the operational discipline of deploying agentic systems without ceding oversight — where trust and verification actually break down at scale.

  5. Most companies are still chasing incremental AI, not transformational AI

    A theme Prommer has been quoted on in CIO.com's 2026 State of the CIO coverage: the gap between where enterprises say they're headed with AI and where their operations actually are.

Suggested Podcast Questions

  1. What does "AI-native operator" actually mean day to day — what changed in how you run We The Flywheel?

  2. You talk about the shift "from CTO to CTAIO" — where's the line between an agent that assists with a process and one that runs it end to end?

  3. Flywheel Studio builds on spec-driven, agentic coding workflows — what was the first operation you fully handed to an agent, and what broke?

  4. How do you decide what not to automate?

  5. If a founder wants to move toward agentic operations, what's the first thing they should do in the next 30 days?

  6. You've spoken about "Responsible AI in Practice" — where do agentic systems fail silently, and how do you catch it before it costs you?
