Talking Points — Agentic Operations

  1. Agentic operations, not agentic chat

    The shift from AI as a conversational assistant to AI as the system that actually runs a business process, end to end, without a human approving every step.

  2. Rebuild, don't bolt on

    Why retrofitting AI onto an existing org chart caps the upside — and what it looks like to design operations around agents from day one instead.

  3. Agents belong on the org chart

    Treating an agent as an accountable unit with defined scope and a clear owner, not just a tool bolted onto a human's workflow.

  4. The real bottleneck is trust, not capability

    Most agentic systems today can already do the work. What's missing is a way to know when to trust the output without re-checking it by hand.

  5. What breaks first

    The failure modes that only show up once you hand real operations to agents — and why they rarely look like the failures people expect.
